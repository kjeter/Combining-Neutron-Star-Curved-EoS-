# Combining-Neutron-Star-Curved-EoS-
I took the graduate course Compact Objects, instructed by Dr. Saavik Ford and Dr. Barry McKernan, while in the CUNY MS in Astrophysics program. For my final project, I combined the equation of state for neutrons in an external magnetic field and for charged fermions, as derived in (Hossain & Mandal 2023), for neutron stars. In this paper, Hossain & Mandeal derived two equations of state for neutron star matter in a curved space-time and under a strong external magnetic field. One equation of state is for neutrons, and the other is for charged fermions. I manipulated the derived equations and converted them to functions of radius. I then developed a Python script that combined the equations of state based on radial depth. For the outer crust, extending from the surface to about 1 km radially in the neutron star (0.9 <= r/Rns <= 1, where Rns is the total radius of the neutron star, taken to be 10 km for this explanation), I used the equation of state for charged fermions. For the inner crust, extending from 0.6 <= r/Rns <= 0.9, I combined the neutron and charged-fermion equations of state. For the core, extending from 0.0 <= r/Rns <= 0.6, I used the neutron equation of state. The code and project write up is provided. 

Citation:
Hossain, Golam Mortuza, and Susobhan Mandal. "Effects of magnetic field on the equation of state in curved spacetime of a neutron star." arXiv preprint arXiv:2312.16589 (2023).
